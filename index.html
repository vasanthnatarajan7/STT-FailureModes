I built the simulation.
Then I ran real audio.
Here's what changed.

Three things the simulation got right in theory — and real API calls broke in practice. STT failure modes you won't find in any benchmark paper.

The model that scores 2.3% on a benchmark is a different model on your audio. Not worse by a little. Worse by enough to matter. Run your actual call recordings before you pick your escalation thresholds. Inheriting someone else's benchmarks is how you end up with a threshold that doesn't match your caller population.

Why does confidence collapse on card numbers? The model is optimised for natural language — words with semantic context, grammatical relationships, predictable sequences. A 16-digit card number has none of these. No semantic meaning. No grammatical anchor. The model has nothing to lean on when a digit sounds ambiguous. Acting on a 0.61 confidence card number is a liability — not a transcription error.

Silence timeout is a tunable parameter, not a system constant. In production, increase max_turn_silence dynamically when the caller is reading structured data — card numbers, account IDs, addresses. Drop it back for conversational turns. AssemblyAI's u3-rt-pro lets you adjust this per session. Most teams set it once at deployment and never touch it again.
What to do about it
→
Run your own audio first
Before setting any threshold, run a sample of your real call recordings through the API. Your caller population, codec, and use case will produce different confidence distributions than any benchmark.
→
Track entity confidence separately
Overall WER and entity-turn confidence are different signals. A call with 0.95 average confidence can still have a 0.61 card number. Log word.confidence per entity turn, not per transcript.
→
Make silence timeout dynamic
Increase max_turn_silence when your NER layer detects the caller is reading structured data. Drop it back for freeform conversation. One value for all turns is the wrong architecture.
→
Escalate before acting on low confidence
The threshold I use: stt_confidence < 0.72 on any entity turn triggers warm transfer. Don't let the AI act on ambiguous payment or account data. That's not caution — it's compliance.
